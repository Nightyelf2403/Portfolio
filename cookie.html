2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small pieces of data that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and provide essential information to website owners.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1. Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the proper functioning of our website and cannot be disabled. They include cookies that allow you to log in, submit forms, and use other essential features.

3.2. Performance Cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website's performance and user experience.

3.3. Functionality Cookies: Functionality cookies enable us to remember your preferences and choices (such as language or region) to provide you with a more personalized experience on our website.

3.4. Third-Party Cookies: We may use third-party cookies, such as those from Google Analytics, to gather statistical information about website usage. These cookies are subject to the respective privacy policies of the third parties providing them.

4. How We Use Cookies

4.1. To ensure the proper functioning of our website.

4.2. To analyze and improve the performance and functionality of our website.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

5.1. Browser Settings: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually find these settings in the "Options" or "Preferences" menu of your browser. You can learn more about managing cookies in your specific browser by visiting the browser's help documentation. 5.2. Third-Party Tools: You can also use third-party tools and browser extensions to manage and control cookies, including opting out of certain tracking cookies.
